Margherita Pizza Recipe

Description

A Margherita pizza is a timeless classic that celebrates simplicity and flavor in every bite. With its thin, crisp crust as a foundation, it's topped with a rich, tangy tomato sauce made from sun-ripened Italian tomatoes. Creamy slices of fresh mozzarella melt into golden pools, blending perfectly with the sauce, while vibrant green basil leaves add a fragrant herbal note. The final drizzle of extra virgin olive oil enhances the ingredients without overpowering them. Each bite is a perfect harmony of textures and tastes — warm, savory, slightly sweet, and unmistakably fresh. It’s a delicious reminder that sometimes, the simplest things are the most satisfying.

Ingredients for dough

    • 2 cups all-purpose flour (or 00 flour for a more authentic texture)
      ¾ cup warm water
      1 tsp active dry yeast
      1 tsp sugar
      1 tsp salt
      1 tbsp olive oil

    • Ingredients for topping

    • ½ cup tomato sauce (preferably made from San Marzano tomatoes)
      150g fresh mozzarella cheese (sliced or torn into pieces)
      Fresh basil leaves (a handful)
      1 to 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
      Salt (to taste)

    • Optional: A pinch of oregano or a sprinkle of Parmesan for extra flavor (though purists might skip this).

    Steps to make dough

    • In a small bowl, mix the warm water, sugar, and yeast. Stir gently and let it sit for 5 to 10 minutes until frothy.
      In a large mixing bowl, combine flour and salt. Add the yeast mixture and olive oil. Mix until a rough dough forms.
      Transfer to a lightly floured surface and knead for 8 to 10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
      Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth or plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm spot for 1 to 1½ hours or until doubled in size.
      Punch down the dough to release air. On a floured surface, stretch or roll it into a 12-inch circle. Place on a baking tray or pizza stone.

    • Assembly and baking

    • Preheat to 250°C (480°F) or as high as your oven goes. If you have a pizza stone, preheat it too.
      Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce evenly over the base, leaving a small border around the edges.
      Distribute the mozzarella pieces evenly over the sauce.
      Transfer the pizza to the oven and bake for 7 to 10 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly and slightly browned.
      Remove from the oven. Add fresh basil leaves and drizzle with extra virgin olive oil.
      Slice and enjoy immediately while hot and melty!
